Background

Service First. Then a Decade in the Field.

Andrew Powers served eight years in the United States Army, completing two combat deployments to Afghanistan. His service came at a cost that most people will never face. He was involved in an IED attack and carries a 100% permanent and total disability rating from the Department of Veterans Affairs. He received an honorable discharge.

After separating from the Army, Andrew transitioned into information technology and spent the next decade working his way through nearly every labor category PSG places. He started at the Help Desk, moved through Desktop Support, Systems Administration, and Network Administration, and earned his way to Network Engineer II. CompTIA A+, Network+, Security+, and Cisco CCNP came along the way. He holds an active NE2 role today. When Andrew screens a Help Desk candidate or evaluates a Network Administrator, he has personally done that work. That is not something a staffing firm owner can fake or learn from a job description.

"I have done every job PSG places. I started at the Help Desk and worked my way to NE2. When I screen a candidate I am not reading from a rubric. I know exactly what the role demands because I have done it."

That decade in the field gave Andrew something most staffing company owners do not have: a genuine technical baseline. When he screens a Network Administrator candidate or evaluates a Help Desk applicant, he is not matching keywords against a job description. He is asking the questions a senior engineer would ask, drawing on the same experience that qualifies him to do the work himself.

Why PSG Exists

A Problem Worth Solving.

Federal IT staffing has a quality problem. Programs accept underqualified candidates because volume recruiters optimize for speed and margin, not technical fit. Workers who cannot perform create downstream costs for primes, contracting officers, and ultimately for the veterans the VA serves.

Andrew founded Powers Staffing Group to address that gap directly. PSG is built on a straightforward premise: one qualified candidate, screened by someone who has done the work, placed on a contract that is priced correctly and compliant from day one. No false starts. No bait-and-switch. No compliance surprises.

The SDVOSB certification is not incidental to the business. The VA is the agency that served Andrew and that serves millions of veterans nationwide. Helping that agency operate with better-qualified IT staff is not just a business opportunity. It is personal.

PSG is owner-operated by design. Andrew answers every call, screens every candidate, and owns every placement relationship. When something goes wrong mid-contract, there is one person to reach and one person with the authority and the motivation to fix it.

How PSG Operates

The Principles Behind Every Placement

These are not marketing statements. They are the operational commitments Andrew holds himself to on every position PSG accepts.

01

Never accept a position PSG cannot fill

No false starts. No presenting unqualified candidates to protect a relationship. If PSG takes the position, PSG fills it.

02

Pull the Wage Determination before pricing

Every position. Every location. WDs change by locality and by year. PSG never prices from memory or a stale rate.

03

Screen technically, not administratively

A candidate who passes the technical screen gets presented. A candidate who does not, does not. The screener is the owner, not a junior recruiter with a keyword checklist.

04

Every worker is a W-2 employee, always

Full SCA benefits, clearance sponsorship, and payroll compliance from day one. No 1099 misclassification. No exceptions.

05

Answer the phone

Owner-operated means the person who knows the contract, the worker, and the prime picks up. No voicemail trees. No account managers. One number.
